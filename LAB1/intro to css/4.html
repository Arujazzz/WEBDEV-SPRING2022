Travel to Uralsk

Uralsk is a city in Western Kazakhstan, on the Ural River. The administrative center of the West Kazakhstan region, located on the northern outskirts of the Caspian lowland.

What to see ...

  • House-Museum of E.I. Pugacheva

    • The Emelyan Pugachev House-Museum is a museum in the city of Uralsk dedicated to the memory of Emelyan Pugachev. It is a hut of the 18th-century Yaik Cossack Pyotr Kuznetsov, the father of the “Yaik Empress” Ustinya Kuznetsova.
    It has been operating since 1991 as a branch of the West Kazakhstan Regional Museum of History and Local Lore.

  • Cathedral of the Archangel Michael

    • Mikhailo-Arkhangelsky Cathedral in Uralsk - the oldest building in the city, its first temple, founded in 1741 on the site of an existing wooden one, consecrated in 1751.
    The oldest Orthodox church in Kazakhstan. Dedicated to the Archangel Michael - the heavenly patron of the Yaitsky Cossack army.

  • Cathedral of Christ the Savior

    • The temple of the Ural and Aktobe diocese of the Kazakh metropolitan district of the Russian Orthodox Church in Uralsk, built in 1891-1907 in memory of the 300th anniversary of the service of the Ural Cossack army in Russia.
    The Urals call the Cathedral of Christ the Savior "Golden".
